A Small Gallery of Looking

Shenzhe Zhu · May 9, 2026

I put a small gallery on the homepage because I feel closer to art than to academia or engineering. These are works I like looking at, and I will keep adding to this page as the gallery changes.

Monet's Wheatstacks

Monet painted his wheatstacks near Giverny around 1890-91, returning to the same simple subject under different light and weather. I like that the painting does not need a dramatic scene. It is just a field, a stack, and a horizon, but the color keeps changing the whole mood.

Monet is my favorite painter, and a lot of it comes down to color. If I zoom in on a small patch, the strokes can look noisy, almost messy. But when I step back, everything falls into place. The colors balance each other so well that the whole painting feels calm and coherent. I find that really impressive.

Monet in Bordighera

Monet visited Bordighera on the Italian Riviera in 1884. The plants, villas, and Mediterranean light are much denser than in the wheatstack paintings. The image feels busy in a way I enjoy.

I like this one because it is almost too much: palms, walls, blue sky, and a lot of green packed together. It is still Monet chasing light, just with the temperature turned up.

A Young Danqing Chen

This portrait is from a very different context. Baoyuan Xia painted Danqing Chen in 1976, before Chen became widely known for the Tibetan Series, his essays, and his public art criticism. I like seeing him here as a young artist, before the later public image.

I also admire Danqing Chen as a writer and thinker. His portraits and realist paintings are very good, but I am even more drawn to the way he talks about life, taste, education, and self-reflection. I love watching Local Perspective (局部) for this reason.
